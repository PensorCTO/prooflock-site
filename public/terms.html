2. Digital Certainty Definition

ProofLock provides "Digital Certainty" through three interconnected pillars:

  • Integrity: Cryptographic proof that a file has not been altered since notarization (SHA-256 hashing).
  • Ownership: Mathematical proof of file association with your wallet identity (digital signatures).
  • Timestamping: Immutable timestamp anchor on the Polygon blockchain network.

Together, these create a Certificate of Authenticity that proves the digital state of a file at a specific point in time, anchored to the Polygon network.

3. The Immutable Monolith

You acknowledge that the Polygon blockchain ledger is permanent and immutable. Once a cryptographic fingerprint is anchored to the blockchain, it exists forever. We cannot delete, edit, or modify any blockchain records. All notarizations are permanent and irreversible.

4. Your Responsibility (The Bridge)

A verification requires two things that YOU control:

  • The Artifact: The exact, unaltered file that matches the cryptographic hash.
  • The Identity: The Wallet/Email credentials used to sign and anchor the proof.

If either component is lost or altered, the verification link is broken and cannot be restored by ProofLock.

You are solely responsible for maintaining secure access to your account and backing up your encrypted vault files.

6. Loss of Link

If you lose the original file or access to your email/wallet credentials, the link to the blockchain record is permanently broken. ProofLock cannot restore it. The blockchain record remains immutable, but without both the file and your credentials, verification is impossible.

7. Best Practice - Backup Encrypted Assets

To guarantee verification, we strongly recommend backing up the encrypted version from your Vault. Storing raw decrypted files allows operating system metadata changes that can permanently break the cryptographic match. Always back up your encrypted vault files, not the original unencrypted files, to maintain cryptographic integrity.
